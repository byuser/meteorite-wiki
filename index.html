Explore the World of Meteorites

Meteorites are fragments of asteroids, the Moon and Mars that survive their fiery plunge through Earth's atmosphere. This wiki is a friendly introduction to what they are, what they're made of, and how scientists tell them apart.

What is a meteorite?

A meteoroid is a small rocky or metallic body travelling through space. When it enters the atmosphere and glows it becomes a meteor (a "shooting star"). Any piece that survives to reach the ground is called a meteorite. Studying meteorites lets us hold pieces of the early Solar System — and even other worlds — in our hands.
