Meteorite Composition

Almost every meteorite is built from a mix of silicate minerals and an iron–nickel metal alloy. The balance between these two ingredients is what separates stony, stony-iron and iron meteorites. Below are the most important minerals and metals you'll meet across this wiki.

Silicates vs. metal

Silicate minerals such as olivine and pyroxene dominate stony meteorites. Iron–nickel metal, made of the alloys kamacite and taenite, dominates iron meteorites. Stony-irons sit in between, with both present in roughly equal amounts.

The Widmanstätten pattern

When an iron meteorite is cut, polished and etched with acid, the intergrowth of kamacite and taenite reveals a unique crisscross structure called the Widmanstätten pattern. It can only form over millions of years of extremely slow cooling deep inside a parent body, so it is a reliable fingerprint of a genuine iron meteorite.
