Is It a Meteorite?

Most rocks people suspect are meteorites turn out to be ordinary Earth rocks or slag — affectionately called "meteorwrongs". No single test is conclusive, but working through the checks below quickly sorts the promising candidates from the rest.

Quick field tests

When in doubt, ask an expert

If a sample passes the encouraging tests — a genuine fusion crust, regmaglypts, unusual density and no vesicles — it is worth a closer look. University geology departments and natural history museums will often examine a promising specimen, and the Meteoritical Bulletin Database is the authoritative record of confirmed meteorites. Avoid destructive tests like cutting or acid-etching until an expert has weighed in.
